The Rich Keep Getting Richer: Are Discrepancies in Broadcast Deals Ruining Club Soccer?

With the Premier League Recently announcing a 3.1 billion dollar profit in TV revenue for the 2021-22 season, will the rest of the world be left in the dust?
~Finn Lincoln

Setting the Stage

A photo of Premier League rivals Arsenal
and Chelsea clashing in the League.
Photo provided by The Independent.

In recent news, a spotlight has been placed on the wealth disparity between England’s top soccer league (the Premier League) and other leagues throughout the globe. The Premier League reported TV revenue of more than 3.1 billion dollars in the 2021-22 season, while La Liga, which generated the second most in the world, earned less than half the TV revenue.

Questions have swirled in the wake of this finding. Does the Premier League deserve to be making so much? Is the quality of the Premier League so high that its watchability is double the value of the next biggest league?

Using statistics and numbers from an advanced soccer statistics statistics measurement website, Football Reference (or FBRef) helps take a deeper dive to look at the discrepancy in quality between leagues. This study specifically looked at England’s Premier League, Spain’s La Liga, Germany’s Bundesliga, Italy’s Serie A, and France’s Ligue 1.

The question becomes, do the numbers back up the notion that the quality of the Premier League is so much higher than that of other leagues? Because the main numbers of focus relate to TV revenue, it is essential to determine what stats prove what league is watchable and exciting.
